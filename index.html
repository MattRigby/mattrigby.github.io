About Me

        What began, for me, as a childhood dream to work in and around athletics, has blossomed into a young career working in various media relations capacities for numerous sports organizations. Here’s a little more about the journey so far:
        Over the course of the past five years, both via my education at the University of Nebraska (upon which I earned a degree in Broadcast Journalism) as well as through my practical employment experiences, I have come to garner a love, understanding, and appreciation for play-by-play duties, and perhaps more importantly, for all of the media relations duties that accompany play-by-play work.
    Through my education at the University of Nebraska, I was able to learn the skills necessary for these duties, and furthermore, I was also lucky enough to have been granted the opportunity to refine these skills, in perfect harmony with my education, by covering various Nebraska athletic teams/programs, both from a play-by-play, and from a journalistic standpoint.
     Outside of my education, I have also been lucky enough to have the opportunity to serve as the play-by-play broadcaster and media relations director for two separate baseball organizations within the Cape Cod Baseball League, the Cotuit Kettleers and the Harwich Mariners. These experiences allowed me to put my education and lifelong interests, passions, and skills to practice in a real-world setting. 
      I am a hard-working, well-read, extremely effective communicator, whose lifelong interest and passion for sports and broadcasting translates well into the adaptability, creativity, and flexibility necessary to any play-by-play and/or media relations role.
